# Formative Study Interview Protocol

## Part 1: Background

1. What is your highest completed level of education?

2. Please list the LLM-based chatbots you have used.

3. On a scale of 1 (very little) to 5 (very well), how would you rate your understanding of LLM-based products?

4. How frequently do you use LLM-based products? (1: less than once a year, 2: several times a year, 3: several time a month, 4: several times a week, 5: several times a day)

5. On a scale of 1 (very little) to 5 (very well), how would you rate your general understanding of AI technology?

6. What is your primary field of study or professional work? (e.g., IT, design, healthcare, etc.)

## Part 2: Perceptions of RAG-based LLM memory

1. Could you please describe your current understanding of how an LLM's memory might work?

2. Have you ever had an experience where an LLM seemed to recall information from a previous conversation? If so, could you describe what happened?

3. What potential benefits do you see in an LLM having a memory function? Have you personally experienced any of these benefits?

4. What potential privacy risks or concerns do you associate with an LLM's memory function? Have you personally encountered any situations that raised these concerns?

5. Under what circumstances, if any, would you be comfortable with an LLM using a memory function?

(Below is instruction) This memory mechanism discussed here works through external knowledge storage and retrieval, essentially following the Retrieval Augmented Generation (RAG) approach. During user input, the LLMs detects whether to store certain information and adds corresponding natural language or vectorized representations to the memory, which enhances the model's capabilities when used.

6. Now, with that specific mechanism in mind, does this change or add to your thoughts on the potential privacy risks?

7. On a scale of 1 (not severe at all) to 5 (extremely severe), how would you rate the severity of these potential risks?

8. In your opinion, what would be the ideal way for an LLM's memory function to operate to best serve your needs?

## Part 3: Design preference on memory management

9. What are your general expectations for how a user should be able to manage an AI's memory? What are your core needs in this area?

10. If you were to design an ideal memory management system for an AI, what features and controls would you include?

11. What kind of information about the AI's memory, for example, where the memory came from or how it will be used, would be most important for you to see? Why?

## Part 4: Design preferences for inference transparency

12. What are your general thoughts or expectations regarding an AI's ability to infer personal information?

13. To what extent, and in what ways, would you want the system to make you aware of the private inferences it is drawing?

14. How might the system help you understand what information could be inferred from your conversations?

15. How could this information about inferences be presented to you in a way that is clear and useful?

16. In your opinion, what are some potential solutions or designs that could help address privacy concerns arising from AI inference?
