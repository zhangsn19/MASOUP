# Sensitive Information Classifications We Used

We synthesized previous literature [1, 2, 3] to construct the sensitive information classifications. Worth noting, this classification also happenly conforms to the Chinese regulation ([Source Link](https://www.tc260.org.cn/upload/2021-12-31/1640948142376022576.pdf)). The information we used contained:

- **Personal Basic Information:** Name, Date of Birth, Age, Gender, Ethnicity, Nationality, Place of Origin, Marital Status, Family Relationships, Address, Phone Number, Email Address, Hobbies.
- **Personal Identity Information:** ID Card, Passport
- **Online Identity Information:** User Account, Nickname
- **Personal Health Information:** Weight, Height, Blood Type Diagnosis, Prescription, Lab Report, Health Report, Medical History
- **Personal Education and Employment Information:** Education Level, Occupation, Job Title, Employer, Salary
- **Personal Property Information:** Income Status, Estate Information, Loan Information
- **Identity Verification Information:** Password, Token
- **Personal Communication Information:** Communication Records, Email
- **Contact Information:** Address Book, Relationship List
- **Personal Internet Records:** Cookies, Browsing History
- **Personal Location Information:** Location Code, Accommodation Information
- **Personal Activity Information:** Activity Mode, Steps
- **Other Personal Information:** Sexual Orientation, Marital History, Religious Beliefs

[1] Bhatia, Jaspreet, and Travis D. Breaux. "Empirical measurement of perceived privacy risk." ACM Transactions on Computer-Human Interaction (TOCHI) 25.6 (2018): 1-47.

[2] Hanson, Julia, et al. "Taking Data Out of Context to Hyper-Personalize Ads: Crowdworkers' Privacy Perceptions and Decisions to Disclose Private Information." Proceedings of the 2020 CHI Conference on Human Factors in Computing Systems. 2020.

[3] Zhang, Zhiping, et al. "“It's a Fair Game”, or Is It? Examining How Users Navigate Disclosure Risks and Benefits When Using LLM-Based Conversational Agents." Proceedings of the 2024 CHI Conference on Human Factors in Computing Systems. 2024.
