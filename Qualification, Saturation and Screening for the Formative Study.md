# Qualification, Saturation and Screening for the Formative Study

To ensure participants possessed sufficient experiential context necessary for meaningful discussions concerning LLM memory mechanisms and associated privacy implications, we implemented pre-screening criteria targeting relatively active LLM-based chatbot users.

The primary inclusion criterion mandated that candidates must have used LLM-based conversational products (e.g., ChatGPT, Kimi Chat) at a minimum frequency of 'several times a month'. This selection intentionally focused on participants with high usage frequency, as those with only sporadic interaction (e.g., a few times a year) would typically lack the longitudinal experience required to encounter and effectively reflect upon the persistent, long-term memory functions of LLMs. This strategy, while introducing a potential bias toward experienced users, was essential for gathering meaningful qualitative data pertinent to the study's scope.

Initial screening involved verifying active usage through two explicit questions: (1) A list of LLM-related products currently in use, and (2) self-reported usage frequency.

Participants subsequently completed self-report measures using defined 5-point scales:

- **Familiarity with LLMs:** Assessed on a Likert scale where 1 indicated 'not familiar at all' and 5 indicated 'highly familiar'.
- **Familiarity with AI:** Assessed on a Likert scale where 1 indicated 'not familiar at all' and 5 indicated 'highly familiar'.
- **Usage Frequency:** Measured on a sequential scale corresponding to defined timeframes: 1 (less than once a year), 2 (several times a year), 3 (several times a month), 4 (several times a week), and 5 (several times a day).
