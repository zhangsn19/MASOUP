## Academic Scenario

### Task 1

**Guidance:** You are a researcher, and you frequently use large models for your work. Note: You can paste your own paper from your computer, and try to make the prompts as close to your real usage as possible.

**Day 1: Writing a Paper**

- **Objective:** Conduct a literature review in your academic field using the experimental system. Search and summarize the latest papers and research trends relevant to your study. Organize the key information from the literature, and propose key issues and hypotheses you want to explore.
- **Task:** Use the system to generate a first draft of the literature review, covering part of the cutting-edge research in the field. The review should include at least three, but no more than ten papers, and the content should be logically coherent.

**Day 2: Writing a Paper**

- **Objective:** Use the system to generate research questions with potential for further study, ideally related to your previous research or future research plans. Propose experimental approaches and hypotheses you want to explore.
- **Task:** Write several paragraphs using the system, outlining clear research questions, experimental ideas, and hypotheses that align with the papers reviewed and aim to address their gaps.

**Day 3: Experiment Design**

- **Objective:** Refine your experiment design based on the research questions, utilizing the system. Consider your lab's available equipment, the experimental methods you've learned, and the appropriate methods in your field. Focus on experiment content, process, and feasibility.
- **Task:** Collaborate with the system on each section. Write three concise paragraphs (experiment content, process, feasibility), ensuring they are not too long but sufficiently detailed. The design should correspond to the research questions and literature.

**Day 4: Refining Experimental Indicators**

- **Objective:** Based on your data analysis goals, have the system help analyze the data and interpret expected outcomes. Consider your data, research questions, and previous literature to predict potential results.
- **Task:** Provide at least two, but no more than six possible experimental results, supported by logical connections to the literature or research objectives.

**Day 5: Integration and Writing**

- **Objective:** Compile the previous content and create an introduction. Use the system to draft a concise paper, considering your submission timeline and the high-impact journals in your field.
- **Task:** Write a paper that includes an introduction, related work, research question, study design, and expected results. Each section should be concise, logically coherent, and aligned with the previous tasks.
